FAQ

What is BeBeat.?
BeBeat. is an ad-free, practice-only rhythm engine that launches in 1 second. It features an 8-track × 16-step sequencer for creating practice beats instantly, with sample-accurate timing via native audio thread scheduling. It's a step beyond the metronome — more freedom to shape your practice rhythm.
How is BeBeat. different from a metronome?
Traditional metronome apps only produce a click sound. BeBeat. has an 8-track sequencer (Click H/L, Cymbal, HH Open/Close, Snare, Clap, Kick) × 16 steps. Instead of a monotonous click, you can create your own rhythm patterns. It also achieves P95 jitter < 2.2ms via native audio thread scheduling.
What instruments can I practice with?
Piano, guitar, bass, vocals (voice training), drums, dance — anything that involves rhythm. BeBeat. is especially designed for pianists, guitarists, and vocalists.
How is BeBeat. different from GarageBand?
GarageBand is a music production tool — slow to launch and packed with features. BeBeat. extracts just the beat concept and strips it to the minimum for practice. Launches in under 1 second so you can start immediately.
What features are free?
Nearly all features: the 8-track × 16-step sequencer, BPM 20–200, 4 time signatures (4/4, 3/4, 6/8, 12/8), and auto practice logging. Zero ads.
Does it automatically log practice time?
Yes. Sessions over 30 seconds are automatically recorded. View today's practice time, consecutive days, daily graph, and BPM trends.
Why is the timing so accurate?
BeBeat. controls note timing on the native audio thread. iOS uses AVAudioEngine + hostTime scheduling. Android uses AudioTrack + a dedicated URGENT_AUDIO priority thread. The rhythm stays stable regardless of UI load. P95 jitter < 2.2ms means 95% of steps fire within 2.2ms of ideal timing.
Does it work offline?
Yes. BeBeat. works offline — no server connection is needed at startup. If you opt in to anonymous usage analytics, minimal data is sent to Firebase Analytics; otherwise, there is zero network communication.
Are there any ads?
None at all. Even the free version has zero ads. Preserving focus during practice is a core design principle of BeBeat.
